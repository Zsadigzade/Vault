# OKRs and execution

## TL;DR

- **Objective:** qualitative direction + motivation (not a number, not a task).
- **Key results:** 3–5 measurable outcomes per O; binary or numeric — not activities.
- **Grading:** 0.7 is good. 1.0 every cycle = you sandbagged.

## Structure

```
Objective: [Direction + Why it matters — one sentence]
  KR 1: [Measurable outcome by date]
  KR 2: [Measurable outcome by date]
  KR 3: [Measurable outcome by date]
```

**Good objective:** "Launch a product experience that earns users' daily trust"
**Bad objective:** "Launch v2 feature set" ← task, not direction

**Good KR:** "Increase D30 retention from 22% to 35% by June 30"
**Bad KR:** "Improve retention" ← not measurable
**Bad KR:** "Ship 5 features" ← activity, not outcome

---

## The rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| 1 objective per team per quarter (max 3) | Focus; more = nothing moves |
| 3–5 KRs per O | Enough to triangulate; too many dilutes |
| KRs are outcomes, not tasks | "Ship X" is a task. "Achieve Y" is a result |
| 0.7 grade is good | 1.0 means too easy; 0.0 means unrealistic |
| OKRs are not performance reviews | Decouple from compensation or gaming starts |
| Stretch OKRs need aspirational framing | "We might achieve 0.6; that's still great progress" |

---

## Grading

Grade at end of quarter: 0.0 – 1.0
- **0.7–1.0** — great
- **0.4–0.6** — okay; learn why
- **0.0–0.3** — missed; root-cause required

Average across KRs = Objective score.

---

## Cascade structure

```
Company OKRs (CEO sets)
  └── Team OKRs (aligned to company O)
        └── Individual OKRs (aligned to team O)
```

Bottom-up works too: team proposes how their work contributes upward. CEO verifies coverage of company objectives.

---

## Weekly check-in (COO / CoS)

Each week, for each KR:
1. **Confidence score:** 1–10 (will we hit it by deadline?)
2. **What moved since last week?**
3. **What's blocking progress?**
4. **What do we need to change?**

A dropping confidence score early is a gift — it means there's still time to intervene.

---

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Fix |
|-------------|-----|
| OKRs as task list | Rewrite KRs as outcomes with metrics |
| Too many objectives | Force-rank; pick one that matters most |
| Sandbagging KRs | Push teams to set uncomfortable targets; 0.7 is the goal |
| Setting and forgetting | Weekly check-ins are mandatory |
| Tying OKRs to bonuses | Creates gaming behaviour; separate them |
| No CEO-level OKRs | Company direction must be set from top down |
