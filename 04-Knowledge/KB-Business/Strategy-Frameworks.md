# Strategy frameworks

## TL;DR

- **Industry attractiveness:** Porter Five Forces (threat of entry, substitutes, supplier/buyer power, rivalry).
- **New space:** Blue Ocean — eliminate/reduce/raise/create (ERRC grid).
- **Portfolio:** BCG matrix (cash cows, stars, question marks, dogs) — use with caution for small cos.

## When to use which

| Question | Tool |
|----------|------|
| Why are margins compressing? | Porter Five Forces |
| Category feels zero-sum? | Blue Ocean ERRC |
| Where to invest vs harvest? | BCG Matrix |
| Where to grow next? | Ansoff Matrix |
| Full situational audit? | SWOT → TOWS |
| Org alignment problem? | McKinsey 7-S |

## Porter Five Forces

Five structural forces that determine industry profitability. Use to diagnose why you can or can't charge premium prices.

| Force | Key question | High threat signals |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Threat of new entry** | How hard is it for newcomers? | Low capital, no IP, no switching costs |
| **Bargaining power of buyers** | Can customers squeeze margins? | Many alternatives, undifferentiated product |
| **Bargaining power of suppliers** | Can vendors raise prices? | Few suppliers, critical input, no substitutes |
| **Threat of substitutes** | What else does the job? | Better alternatives exist outside the industry |
| **Competitive rivalry** | How intense is current competition? | Many equal rivals, slow growth, high fixed costs |

**Implication:** High forces everywhere = commoditized, low-margin market. Find one force to counter with moat (network, brand, switching cost, IP, scale).

## Blue Ocean ERRC Grid

Don't compete in existing markets — create uncontested space. Four actions to reconstruct market boundaries:

| Action | Question |
|--------|---------|
| **Eliminate** | Which factors that the industry competes on can be eliminated entirely? |
| **Reduce** | Which factors should be reduced well below the industry standard? |
| **Raise** | Which factors should be raised well above the industry standard? |
| **Create** | Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered? |

**Output:** New value curve that looks different from all competitors. If your curve looks identical to a competitor's — you're in a red ocean.

Source: Kim & Mauborgne, *Blue Ocean Strategy* (Harvard Business Review Press)

## BCG Matrix

Portfolio analysis across **market growth rate** (vertical) and **relative market share** (horizontal):

| Quadrant | Label | Strategy |
|----------|-------|---------|
| High growth, high share | **Star** | Invest to maintain leadership |
| Low growth, high share | **Cash cow** | Harvest for cash; minimal investment |
| High growth, low share | **Question mark** | Invest selectively or divest |
| Low growth, low share | **Dog** | Divest or wind down |

**Caution for small cos:** BCG assumes you can define market share. Apply the logic (allocate resources by growth potential × competitive position) even without formal quadrant labels.

## Ansoff Matrix

Where to grow next — 2×2 of existing vs new products × existing vs new markets:

| | **Existing product** | **New product** |
|---|---|---|
| **Existing market** | Market penetration (lowest risk) | Product development |
| **New market** | Market development | Diversification (highest risk) |

Default growth path: penetration → product dev or market dev → diversification only when core is mature.

## SWOT → TOWS

SWOT is not a strategy; TOWS converts it into strategic options:

- **SO (Strengths + Opportunities)** — how to use strengths to capture opportunities
- **ST (Strengths + Threats)** — how to use strengths to defend against threats
- **WO (Weaknesses + Opportunities)** — how to overcome weaknesses to capture opportunities
- **WT (Weaknesses + Threats)** — minimize weaknesses and avoid threats (defensive)

## McKinsey 7-S (org alignment)

When strategy isn't executing — check alignment across 7 interdependent elements:

**Hard elements (easier to define):** Strategy, Structure, Systems
**Soft elements (harder to change):** Shared Values, Skills, Staff, Style

If strategy changes but Structure, Staff, or Systems don't — execution fails. All 7 must move together.
